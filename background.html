Synopsis

A brief review of where I've been and what I've learned

Formal Education

I am currently a second year master's student in mathematics at the University of British Columbia, in Vancouver, Canada. Since September 2019, I have taken classes and researched evolutionary discounting schedules under the supervision of Christoph Hauert. My master's thesis, to do with evolutionary pressures to prefer small, more immediate rewards over larger, more delayed rewards, is under construction. Though far from accessible, the adventurous reader can find more on Github.

Before coming to UBC, I studied economics and mathematics at the University of Ottawa. My studies culminated in the writing of my honour's project, which has even (to my pleasant surprise) been cited a handful of times! Between my bachelor's and master's degrees, I have taken courses covering:

  • Math essentials: Multivariable calculus. Linear algebra. Real and complex analysis. Groups, rings, fields, and Galois theory. Measure theory, integration, and functional analysis. Probability and stochastic processes.
  • Applied math and economics: Micro and macroeconomic theory. Monetary theory and policy. Optimal transport with applications in economics. Combinatorial, classical, and evolutionary game theory. Quantum information theory, computing, and algorithms.

Work Experience

Through university positions, co-op programs, and interships I have worked steadily since 2014. By making a positive contribution to the teams I have worked on, each experience has in turn rewarded me with skills and knowledge which continue to serve me to the present. Though in some ways very different, my work experiences are unified by the theme of communication. Whereas school has taught me rigorous thinking and analysis, working with others has reinforced the important point that information not effectively communicated may as well not exist.

  • Teaching assistant (UBC): Functioned as a point of contact between students, university administration, and professors. Graded assignments, midterms, and final exams. Provided individual tutoring and facilitated group problem sessions and specialized course help sessions.
  • Government of Canada: Worked in multiple federal departments such as Indigenous and Northern Affairs Canada. Drafted reports, performed research, and communicated findings in both oral and written formats for subject matter experts. Produced research tools in excel, Python, and SageMath.
  • IPEA: Worked as a research assistant to academics in London, Ottawa, and Rio de Janeiro. Accessed Brazilian labour data, generated summary statistics, and performed regression analyses in Stata.
  • Parliament of Canada: Worked as a page, serving Members of Parliament, Clerks, and political staff to ensure the routine proceedings of Parliament. Responsibilities included memorizing the names, faces, and party affiliations of all 308 members, as well as the collection and dissemination of confidential and sensitive documents in a non-partisan, bilingual fashion.

Personal Interests

I have always been interested in learning languages. Throughout my time in Ottawa, I took advantae of the opportunities afforded by the university's bilingualism, taking about a third of my courses in French.
